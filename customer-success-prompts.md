# Customer Success Prompts — Level 1

Paste-and-ask prompts for ChatGPT, Claude, or any chat AI. Each one is built for a specific job — copy the prompt, paste the transcript or email thread above or below it, and you'll get usefully structured output.

These are deliberately simple. Level 1 in the Medium piece is the floor, not the ceiling. The point: even at this level — no integrations, no skills, no scheduling — you can get real work done.

## After a customer meeting

**Extract real commitments**

```
You are reviewing a customer meeting transcript I'm pasting below. Extract every concrete commitment that was made and by whom — both my team's commitments and the customer's. For each one, note: who committed, what they committed to, by when (if mentioned), and what would tell us it was done. Skip anything that's just "we'll think about it." Only real commitments.

Transcript:
[paste transcript here]
```

**Find the concern underneath the request**

```
Read the meeting transcript below carefully. List the three things that worried the customer most — but go past what they literally said. For each one, give me: the surface complaint they raised, the underlying concern you think it points to, and the evidence in the transcript that supports your reading. Mark anything where you're guessing.

Transcript:
[paste transcript here]
```

**Draft the follow-up email**

```
Draft a follow-up email to the primary customer contact based on the transcript below. Confirm what we agreed, what they asked us to look into, and what we owe them by when. Five sentences maximum. Match the tone of the meeting — warm, businesslike. Don't add anything we didn't actually discuss.

Transcript:
[paste transcript here]
```

## Reading a transcript more carefully

**Spot what wasn't said**

```
This is a transcript from a customer call. I want you to read it and tell me what's conspicuously absent — topics the customer avoided, questions they didn't ask, agreement that felt forced, anything that suggests there's more going on than what's on the surface. Be specific. Quote moments that triggered your reading.

Transcript:
[paste transcript here]
```

**Catch the tone shift**

```
Read the transcript below. Compare the customer's tone in the first third with their tone in the last third. Did anything shift — engagement, candour, mood, urgency? If yes, when did it shift and what triggered it? If no, say so plainly.

Transcript:
[paste transcript here]
```

## Drafting communications

**Internal escalation note**

```
Based on the transcript below, draft a short escalation note for our internal #customer-success channel. Audience: my colleagues who don't know this customer. Cover: who they are in one line, what changed in this meeting, why it needs attention now, and what I'm proposing we do. Under 100 words. Don't make it sound dramatic — accurate is better than urgent.

Transcript:
[paste transcript here]
```

**Renewal-narrative draft**

```
I'm pasting below a quarter's worth of meeting notes for one customer. Draft a single one-paragraph narrative for a renewal conversation: what they came in needing, what we've delivered, where we've stumbled and recovered, and what the next quarter's bet should be. Keep it honest — don't dress up the stumbles. Five sentences max.

Notes:
[paste notes here]
```

## Customer health

**Diagnose disengagement**

```
I'm pasting below the last two months of email and Slack interactions with a customer who's gone quieter than usual. Look for patterns: response-time changes, who's still engaged versus who's gone silent, topics that used to come up but no longer do, requests that haven't been followed up on. Tell me what you see and what you'd watch for next.

Interactions:
[paste content here]
```

## When you've outgrown these

Each of these prompts assumes you're pasting context into a fresh chat. That works for one meeting, one customer, one thread. The moment you find yourself pasting the same setup into every prompt — *"this customer is on the Pro plan, the primary contact is Anna, we shipped X last month"* — that's the system telling you it needs persistent access to your data instead of a one-off paste.

That's level two. See [`connect-in-parallel-mcp.md`](connect-in-parallel-mcp.md).
